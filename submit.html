SPADE 2025 - Call for Papers & Instructions for Authors

Topics of Interest

We welcome submissions on, but not limited to, the following topics:

Detection and Analysis of Speech Pathologies

  • Techniques for identifying voice and speech disorders
  • Speech biomarkers for neurodegenerative disease detection
  • Early diagnosis of conditions like dementia through speech analysis
  • Assessing severity of speech impairments such as dysarthria

Speech Technology for Affected Populations

  • Customized ASR systems for individuals with speech impairments
  • Assistive technologies for communication enhancement
  • Speech synthesis tailored to specific conditions and age groups
  • Tools for improving daily life for those with speech disorders

Data and Methodology

  • Data collection methods for speech pathology research
  • Annotation techniques for creating robust datasets
  • Benchmarks for evaluating speech pathology detection systems
  • Adapting foundation models for speech disorder analysis

Submission Guidelines

We invite authors to submit their original research or impactful previously published work under two tracks:

  • Original Research Track: Submissions should present new, unpublished work. Accepted papers will be included in the IEEE Xplore Digital Library.
  • Showcase Track: Submissions may include previously published work that is impactful and relevant to the workshop topics. These papers will be presented at the workshop but will not be included in IEEE Xplore.

Submission Format

Submissions must follow the IEEE format, with a maximum of 4 pages, plus 1 additional page for references. Authors can use the following templates for preparing their submissions:

  • LaTeX Template
  • Word Template

All papers must be submitted through the submission site:

Submission link: https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/SPADE2025/

Presentation Format

The workshop will feature a mix of oral and poster paper presentations, providing opportunities for in-depth discussion and networking. Oral presentations will offer detailed discussions and interactive Q&A, while the poster session will facilitate informal discussions and networking.
